Prof. Wole Soyinka

A Nigerian author, best known as a playwright and poet.

video of wole soyinka

Biography

Wole Soyinka was born on July 13, 1934, in Abeokuta, Nigeria. He is a renowned playwright, poet, and essayist who has made significant contributions to literature and human rights advocacy. Soyinka's works often explore themes of political oppression, cultural identity, and social justice.

Soyinka studied at the University of Ibadan and later at the University of Leeds in England

    Achievements
  • First African to win the Nobel Prize in Literature (1986)
  • Imprisoned for his political activism during Nigeria's civil war
  • Founded the Theatre of the Absurd in Nigeria
  • Advocate for democracy and human rights in Africa

Notable Works

  • "A Dance of the Forests" (1960)
  • "Death and the King's Horseman" (1975)
  • "The Lion and the Jewel (1960)"
  • "The Trials of Brother Jero (1960)"

Legacy

Wole Soyinka's impact on literature and society is profound. His works continue to be studied and performed worldwide, and he remains an influential voice in the fight for justice and human rights. Soyinka's legacy is a testament to the power of literature to inspire change and challenge oppression.
